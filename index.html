Our purpose: Why Syria?

Syria, which was a melting pot of cultures once, attracting more tourists than even Australia or Singapore, is facing the largest humanitarian crisis globally in recent times.

As you read this, about 1.7 million Syrian children have lost their access to basic food, shelter, education and security. Today, over 2.3 million Syrians are living as refugees in other countries.

The ongoing civil war, which began over 6 years ago, has left little choice for the survivors and they are forced to start their lives over as refugees and struggle to fend for their families. This is a call for help. To the world, to us.

STOP. NOTICE. ACT.

About MSF

Doctors Without Borders/ Médecins Sans Frontières (MSF) is an international, independent, medical humanitarian organisation that delivers emergency aid to people affected by armed conflict, epidemics, natural disasters and exclusion from healthcare. MSF offers assistance to people based on need, irrespective of race, religion, gender or political affiliation.

MSF is among the few organisation which are working closely at the grassroots of war-torn Syria and providing unconditional medical support to the people affected by war. It also supports and facilitates relief & rehabilitation to the countless refugees left without a home or any supplies to support their family.
